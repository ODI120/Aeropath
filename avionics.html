Who Are Avionics Engineers?

The electronic systems of an aeroplane, such as the flight control, navigation, communication, and monitoring systems, are the focus of avionics engineers.

Pathways to Become an Avionics Engineer

    National Diploma (ND) → Higher National Diploma (HND A&P and Avionics)
    120-week Standard Programme (A\&P)
    Abridged AME Programme (for experienced technicians)

Requirements

    Pass medical and aptitude test
    At least 5 credits in WAEC/NECO including Physics, Maths, and English
    ND or HND in related field (for diploma route)

Average Duration

    ND + HND+1yr IT+ Lpc: \~6 years
    Standard Programme: 2.5 years (120 weeks)
    Abridged Programme: 6–12 months

Average Salary (Nigeria)

    Entry level: ₦300,000–₦500,000/month
    Mid-career: ₦800,000–₦1.5M/month
    Senior Captains: ₦2M+/month

Job Opportunities

    Commercial airlines
    Charter companies
    Government aviation
    Private/business jets
