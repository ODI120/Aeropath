Who Are A&P Engineers?

Who Are Aircraft Maintenance Engineers? The skilled experts in charge of examining, fixing, and maintaining aircraft to make sure they are safe and airworthy are known as Aircraft Maintenance Engineers (AMEs). Their work, which requires in-depth understanding of aircraft systems, construction, and operations, is essential to aviation safety. There are two primary specialisations for AMEs: 1. Powerplant and Airframe (A&P): These engineers are in charge of the engines (powerplant) and the aircraft's airframe. They fix things like engines, fuel systems, landing gear, and hydraulics.

Pathways to Become an AME

    National Diploma (ND) → Higher National Diploma (HND A&P)
    120-week Standard Programme (A\&P)
    Abridged AME Programme (for experienced technicians)

Requirements

    Pass medical and aptitude test
    At least 5 credits in WAEC/NECO including Physics, Maths, and English
    ND or HND in related field (for diploma route)
    Enroll in an NCAA-approved maintenance program (for 120-week or abridged route)
    Must pass regulatory exams and practical assessments

Average Duration

    ND + HND+1yr IT+ Lpc: \~6 years
    Standard Programme: 2.5 years (120 weeks)
    Abridged Programme: 6–12 months

Certifications

    Basic AMEL (Aircraft Maintenance Engineer License) by NCAA
    Type Ratings for specific aircraft models
    B1 License: For A&P

Average Salary (Nigeria)

    Entry level: ₦250,000–₦400,000/month
    Certified technician: ₦500,000–₦800,000/month
    Senior engineer: ₦1M+/month

Job Opportunities

    Aircraft Maintenance Organizations (AMOs)
    Military and Government fleets
    Aircraft parts companies and MROs
    International carriers (with valid licenses)
