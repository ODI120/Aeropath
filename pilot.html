Pilot

Who Are Pilots?

Pilots are trained professionals who operate aircraft to transport passengers or cargo. This career demands high responsibility, precision, and decision-making skills. They are in charge of safely operating the aircraft, controlling in-flight amenities, coordinating with air traffic control, and reacting to emergencies like bad weather. To guarantee that they can manage both simple and complicated flight situations, pilots must complete extensive training and certification. They play a vital role in guaranteeing dependable, effective, and safe air travel.

Flying Course Breakdown

    Private Pilot License (PPL) – Basic flight training
    Commercial Pilot License (CPL) – Professional-level certification
    Instrument Rating – For flying under IFR conditions
    Multi-Engine Rating – For aircraft with more than one engine
    Type Rating – For specific aircraft models

Requirements

    Minimum age: 17 (PPL), 18 (CPL)
    High school certificate (preferably with Physics and Maths)
    Pass medical fitness (Class 1 for CPL)

Average Duration

    18–24 months (depending on flight hours and weather conditions)

Average Salary (Nigeria)

    Entry level: ₦300,000–₦500,000/month
    Mid-career: ₦800,000–₦1.5M/month
    Senior Captains: ₦2M+/month

Job Opportunities

    Commercial airlines
    Charter companies
    Government aviation
    Private/business jets
