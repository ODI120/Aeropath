Who Are Air Traffic Controllers?

Air Traffic Controllers ensure the safe and efficient movement of aircraft both on the ground and in the air. They provide instructions, clearances, and monitor aircraft to prevent collisions. They advise pilots on when and where to land, take off, or alter their course in order to prevent collisions and maintain smooth traffic flow. Area control centres, approach control facilities, or control towers are where ATCs operate. Their work, which demands intense focus, prompt decision-making, and great communication skills, is essential to aircraft safety. A related field under ATC is Aeronautical Information Services (AIS)—responsible for collecting, managing, and distributing essential flight data such as NOTAMs (Notices to Airmen), aeronautical charts, and publications that aid navigation and operational decision-making.
